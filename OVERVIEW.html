TrendRadar · Project Overview

A daily trend‑intelligence pipeline for retail.

It watches what's trending across the internet and turns it into what a store should stock — automatically, every day, unattended.

The feedback was: “it's just a database.” It no longer is. It's an automated, multi‑platform system that measures how fast trends move, names the products behind them, and is accumulating the history a predictor will need.

What's built

Multi-platform collection
Live
TikTok hashtags, YouTube trending videos, and Pinterest shopping searches (nails, outfit ideas, dinner ideas) — each behind one shared schema, isolated so one platform failing never stops the run.
Momentum engine
Live
Every trend gets a velocity, an acceleration, and a lifecycle stage from its own accumulating history. Today: 34 rising, 82 cresting, 133 dormant. rising cresting declining dormant
Retail intelligence
Live
Each durable trend maps to product categories to stock, plus specific named products mined from the videos themselves (Cyberpunk 2077, GamerSupps, Pandora) — ranked into a marketing shortlist by trend momentum.
Creators & cross-platform corroboration
Live
Top creators per category, and a linker that flags a topic trending on more than one platform at once — a stronger signal than any single feed.
Forward-growth predictor
Experimental
A model trained only on real captured data, with its accuracy tracked openly over time. Honest status below — it's a prototype, built now so it sharpens as history grows.
Live dashboard
Live
Eight views — trends, analytics graphs, the marketing shortlist, retailer view, influencers, cross-platform, prediction, and an about page — filterable by window, platform, category, and stage.
Real TikTok Shop sales data (EchoTik)
Ready
The highest-value layer — actual products tied to real demand — is fully wired and dormant, waiting on the subscription key. It switches on with one credential; nothing else to build.

Honest status

Solid

  • Reliability: 22 days unbroken, running itself.
  • Breadth: three platforms, cleanly extensible — Pinterest was added in a day.
  • The core deliverable: trends → products a store can act on, today.

Still early (by design)

  • Prediction: accuracy ~0.56 (near chance). Curve shape alone isn't enough yet — needs more history, tracked transparently rather than faked.
  • Youngest platforms: YouTube and Pinterest have only days of history; their momentum sharpens with time.

What's next

Turn on real sales data.
The EchoTik integration is built and waiting on the key — the moment it clears, the marketing shortlist goes from “products people are talking about” to “products people are actually buying.” After that: let the history deepen so the predictor earns its keep.
